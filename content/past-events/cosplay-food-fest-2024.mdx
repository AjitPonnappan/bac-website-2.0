With enthusiastic support from
<a href="https://www.bengaluru.in.emb-japan.go.jp/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">The Consulate-General of Japan in Bengaluru,</a>
BAC hosted a Cosplay Show as part of the **Japan Food Festival** on November 10, 2024.

The festival celebrated the food culture and flavours of Japan featuring around 20 Japanese restaurants and food companies. The guests had a chance to enjoy the cuisine with the lineup ranging from
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sushi" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Sushi</a>
to Japanese alcohol.
There were stalls selling Japanese groceries,
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karaage" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Karaage</a>,
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manj%C5%AB" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Manju</a>,
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Onigiri" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Onigiri</a>,
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fried_shrimp" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Ebi Fry</a>,
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katsu_curry" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Katsu Curry</a>,
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kakig%C5%8Dri" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Kakigori</a>,
various types of noodles like
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ramen" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Ramen</a>
and
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soba" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Soba</a>
and delicious desserts like
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daifuku" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Daifuku</a>
and
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warabimochi" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Warabimochi</a>.
To provide relief from the heat, there were innovative drinks which brought a unique spin to traditional flavours like
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matcha" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Matcha</a>
and
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barley_tea" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Mugicha</a>.
The event also had the attendees witness a glimpse of the food and drink culture of Japan with a Fish Filleting showcase for
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sushi" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Sushi</a>
and a
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_tea_ceremony" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Chanoyu</a>
ceremony. The vibrant event brought an end with the magic of cosplay in front of a lively audience of about 300 people.

The event ended with 20 cosplayers from BAC taking the stage, each embodying iconic characters from anime, manga, and gaming. From the moment they stepped on the stage, they captivated the crowd with passionate performances. Each cosplayer recited famous lines with charm, striking signature poses and mimicking the unique mannerisms of their characters, transporting the audience straight into the worlds they represented. The crowd was mesmerized, cheering for their favorite characters and admiring the costumes. The energy of the evening was contagious, with every cosplayer and attendee feeling the camaraderie and shared excitement.
